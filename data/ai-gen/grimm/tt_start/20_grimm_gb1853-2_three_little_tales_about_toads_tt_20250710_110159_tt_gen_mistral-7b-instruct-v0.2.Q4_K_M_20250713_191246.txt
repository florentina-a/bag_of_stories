Child was pleased with this; and the next day, and every day when she sat there with her basin, and the Toad did not make its appearance, she used to call it thus:-
 
"Toadie, Toadie, quickly come;

And milk before you set!" II.
But one fine morning, the little Toad did not come. She waited a long time, but still it did not come. This made her uneasy. At length she called out to it with a very displeased tone:-

  "Toadie, Toadie, where are you?
  
Why have you not come to me?

I have milk and bread, I have,

And you have not come to me!"

III.
No answer came, and she began to cry, and to think that perhaps the Toad had gone away. When the sun had risen high, she went to the well to draw water, and there, in a corner, she saw the little Toad sitting quite still, with its mouth open, and looking very pale.

  "Toadie, Toadie, are you ill?

Why do you sit there so pale?

I will fetch you some water,

And a little piece of bread!"

IV.
But the Toad did not move; it could not swallow its little tongue, which was swollen, and it could not speak. It looked so ill, that the little girl took it up in her hand, and carried it gently into the house.

V.
